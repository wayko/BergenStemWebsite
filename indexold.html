History of STEM Student Union

In the Summer of 2012, Belal Ibrahim and Jeyson Flores were given internship opportunities under the guidance of Dr. Joseph Sivo. Their job was to set the groundwork for a building conversion on campus to an off the grid workshop. BCC donated the old softball garage (the one next to the tennis courts), which will eventually become energy independent. The workshop will provide its own electricity, to power various tools, and its own heating for the winter.

The first project was to build a wind turbine, which would be the main source of power for the off the grid blockhouse. Jeyson invited a few of his friends (also engineering majors) and quickly it became apparent that there was interest among the student population in alternative energies, and engineering projects. The small internship that involved only two students, now expanded to many who volunteered their time, simply for their love of science and educating themselves. By the end of summer 2012, there were approximately 10 members, and already in the works plans to work on several project ideas. In the works was also the creation of STEM student union, a recognized BCC club by fall 2013.

The club has grown immensely over the past 2 years. Projects are still developing in STEM under the central guidance of Dr. Sivo, as well as a few other professors who have joined the STEM team (prof. Tartaglia, prof. Sontag, prof. Balzarette, prof. King etc.). The club which used to work out of S315 (which is still headquarters) has expanded to the use of the manufacturing lab (TEC 101) for its many projects. Some of the ongoing projects in STEM include Wind Turbine, Solar Concentrator, UAV team (quad copter), Pollination Survey, Solar Tracker project, CAD Team, Algae Biodiesel, Electric Car Project, Electric Car conversion.

The project logbook supports hours of flying in real world environments; completing several flight tests, including climb rate, maneuverability in weather conditions, and emergency procedures among other flight and support systems.

Purpose of the Club: STEM Student Union

  • Foster Collaboration between existing student of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics on multidisciplinary projects and events.
  • Educate the college community on the importance of each individual discipline as they collaborate to reach a common goal.
  • Create a more substantial and coordinated presence at BCC clubs days and open houses to help attract and encourage more S.T.E.M majors.
